St Mark's Basilica

Apr 18, 2014

This morning Rachel and I took a walking tour of St Mark's Basilica. Before entering the church, we had about an hour walk around the area with the tour guide pointing out other sights - like the Rialto Bridge and Marco Polo's house. St Mark's Basilica was the highlight, of course. The inside was massive and the mosaic work was amazing. We also got to go upstairs to the museum to see the horses and look out over the plaza.

After the tour, we caught the water bus back to the train station and boarded the train for Rome. This was the first truly high-speed train we have ridden on this trip, reaching a maximum speed of about 250 km/h. At top speed it was a challenge to walk around the car.

Once in Rome, we checked into the hotel, ate some pizza and now are resting before our big day tomorrow. We are going on a tour of the Vatican tomorrow and I am pretty excited to see it all.

Have you ever been to Rome? We are here until Tuesday, what should we do? Thanks for sharing.

Rachel and I spent most of April 2014 in Europe. It was an incredible trip that took us to parts of The Netherlands, Germany, Austria, Italy, Switzerland and France. I look forward to the day when we retrace our steps and do it all again.
